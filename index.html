As a director

first.

My directing style is focused on character and story. I love working with performers to find the best nuggets — always a push and pull between what's on the page and what someone brings to the role.

Happy set = good work. If people feel trusted to do their job, they usually do it well. And the work shows it.

No house style. Whatever genre, pacing, or medium the story demands — short film, spot, doc — it doesn't matter.

As an editor
Art and
science.

Editing is where you make the interesting choices that really bring a story to life. Knowing your tools cold means you focus on the "why" instead of the "how."

I've cut brand spots, feature doc teasers, short films, music videos, and social campaigns. The throughline: finding the cut that feels inevitable in retrospect.

Range matters. Big brands, agencies, nonprofits, startups — always looking for what matters most to the audience.

20+
20+ years.
Still having fun.

I've been a writer slash director slash editor slash a lot of other things for many years. I mostly work in video, but I've dabbled in other forms. Dabbled with the best of 'em.

Like many a Chicago dweller, I found my footing in short-form storytelling through sketch comedy and improv. After realizing I was no good at corporate lackey-ness, I parlayed that into co-owning Big Teeth Productions.

I've been around long enough to know you need a creative vision — but you also need to know how to adapt. My goal is to get as close to that vision as possible while skiing around the moguls of opinion, fact, and schedule changes.

If all that has intrigued you enough to want to know more, or better yet hire me — good for me, and better for you.
